# Companion Dialog Samples

*Tone reference for the player/companion dynamic. These are vibes, not final scripts.*

The companion is small (20x32px), resourceful, and opinionated. Think somewhere between a loyal droid and a feral cat — helpful on their own terms, occasionally inconvenient, always honest. They communicate in short chirps/beeps that the player "translates" (or full speech — TBD). These samples assume speech.

The player character is dry, competent, and slightly tired of everything.

---

## Exploration Banter

*Triggered by entering areas, finding things, or idle time.*

---

**[Entering The Barrens for the first time]**

**Companion:** This place smells like hot sand and regret.
**Player:** That's just sand. The regret is mine.

---

**[Finding a skeleton near a rusted vehicle]**

**Companion:** Think they made it?
**Player:** ...They're a skeleton.
**Companion:** I meant spiritually.

---

**[Companion uses gather animation on a strange plant]**

**Companion:** I think this is edible.
**Player:** You think *everything* is edible.
**Companion:** And I've been right most of the time.
**Player:** You were in the med bay for two days.
**Companion:** Most. Of the time.

---

**[Idle too long in Dustport]**

**Companion:** Are we waiting for something or have you just... stopped?
**Player:** I'm thinking.
**Companion:** Oh. Should I come back tomorrow?

---

**[Entering the Overgrown Vaults]**

**Player:** Stay close.
**Companion:** I was going to say that to you.
**Player:** You're eight inches tall.
**Companion:** Height is not a factor in courage.
**Player:** It's a factor in reaching things.

---

**[Finding a locked door in the Vaults]**

**Companion:** I could fit through that crack.
**Player:** And then what? Open it from the other side?
**Companion:** No, I'd just be on the other side. Alone. In the dark.
**Player:** So why—
**Companion:** I didn't say it was a *good* plan.

## Combat Banter

*Triggered during or after fights.*

---

**[Combat starts — bugs]**

**Companion:** Bugs.
**Player:** I see them.
**Companion:** I hate bugs.
**Player:** I know.
**Companion:** Just making sure we're on the same page.

---

**[Player takes a big hit]**

**Companion:** You okay?
**Player:** Fine.
**Companion:** You're literally on fire.
**Player:** I said fine.

---

**[After a tough fight]**

**Player:** That was close.
**Companion:** You say that every time.
**Player:** It's close every time.

---

**[Companion attacks an enemy]**

**Companion:** Got one!
**Player:** ...That was already dead.
**Companion:** Got one faster, then.

---

**[Fighting robots in the ruins]**

**Companion:** These ones are still running. Who's maintaining them?
**Player:** That's what we're here to find out.
**Companion:** I was hoping you'd say "nobody" and we'd leave.

---

**[Boss encounter — Dust Warrior]**

**Companion:** That one's big.
**Player:** Yep.
**Companion:** Like, really big.
**Player:** I have eyes.
**Companion:** I'm just saying, we could also not do this.

## NPC Interaction Banter

*Companion reacts to conversations with NPCs.*

---

**[After talking to Morrow, the black market dealer]**

**Companion:** I don't trust him.
**Player:** Nobody trusts him. That's his whole thing.
**Companion:** Then why are we buying from him?
**Player:** Because he has the thing we need.
**Companion:** That's how they get you.

---

**[After Kael gives you a job]**

**Companion:** Seems straightforward.
**Player:** It's never straightforward.
**Companion:** Can we pretend? Just this once?

---

**[Senna (fruit merchant) gives vague information]**

**Player:** She knows more than she's saying.
**Companion:** Everyone on this planet knows more than they're saying. It's the local hobby.

---

**[After an NPC lies to you — player knows]**

**Player:** That was a lie.
**Companion:** Oh, absolutely. Which part?
**Player:** All of it.
**Companion:** Even the part about the weather?
**Player:** ...Okay, maybe not the weather.

## Companion Personal Moments

*Quieter beats. These earn the funny stuff.*

---

**[Night time in camp]**

**Companion:** Do you think we'll actually find what you're looking for?
**Player:** I don't know.
**Companion:** ...
**Player:** But I'm not stopping.
**Companion:** I know. That's why I'm still here.

---

**[After a story revelation]**

**Companion:** This is bigger than us.
**Player:** Most things are.
**Companion:** That's not what I — I mean, this matters. What we do next matters.
**Player:** Yeah. I know.

---

**[Companion's personal quest — opening up]**

**Companion:** I wasn't always like this, you know.
**Player:** Like what?
**Companion:** Small. Loud. Following a stranger around a desert.
**Player:** You're not following me. You chose to come.
**Companion:** ...Yeah. I guess I did.

---

**[Late game — player is exhausted]**

**Player:** I'm tired.
**Companion:** I know.
**Player:** Not fishing for a pep talk.
**Companion:** Good, because I don't have one. I'm tired too. But we're almost there.
**Player:** How do you know?
**Companion:** Because if we weren't, I'd have quit by now.

## Contextual One-Liners

*Short companion barks for specific moments.*

| Trigger | Companion Line |
|---------|---------------|
| Player opens an empty chest | "Wow. Air. My favorite." |
| Player walks into a wall repeatedly | "Are you okay? Genuinely asking." |
| Player buys something expensive | "That was all our money. ALL of it." |
| Player sells a weapon | "Goodbye, friend. You were adequate." |
| Entering a shop | "Don't let me buy anything. I mean it. ...Unless it's shiny." |
| Finding a save point / camp | "Oh thank god." |
| Player heals | "Maybe dodge next time?" |
| Low health | "Your insides are becoming outsides." |
| Enemy drops rare loot | "GRAB IT GRAB IT GRAB IT" |
| Entering The Murk | "This swamp has a personality and it's hostile." |
| Finding a hidden area | "I *told* you that wall looked suspicious." |
| Companion gets hit | "OW. Remember me as I was." |
| Companion recovers | "Never mind. I'm fine. Forget the eulogy." |
| Player idles near a cliff | "Please don't." |
| Returning to Dustport | "Home sweet...well. Home adjacent." |

## Dialog System Notes

- Banter should trigger based on context, not randomly — the humor lands because it's *specific* to what just happened
- Never interrupt gameplay-critical moments with jokes. Comedy comes in the breath after tension, not during it
- The companion should occasionally be wrong, annoyed, or scared — they're a character, not a quip machine
- The quiet moments (camp, personal quest) make the funny moments funnier. Earn the bit.
- Repetition should be avoided — each line plays once, or has a long cooldown. Nothing kills humor faster than hearing the same joke twice
- The player character is funny because they're *understated*, not because they're trying to be funny
